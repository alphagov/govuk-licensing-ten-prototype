Submit a temporary event notice (TEN) if you want to carry out a ‘licensable activity’ on unlicensed premises in England or Wales.

Licensable activity includes:

  • selling alcohol
  • serving alcohol to members of a private club
  • providing entertainment, such as music, dancing or indoor sporting events
  • serving hot food or drink between 11pm and 5am

You will also need a TEN if a particular licensable activity is not included in the terms of your existing licence, for example holding a wedding reception at a community centre.

You must apply at least 10 clear working days before your event.

Applying takes around 5 minutes and costs £21. You need to be at least 18 years old to apply.

Before you start

To be eligible for a TEN, your event needs to:

  • have fewer than 500 people attending at all times, including staff running the event
  • last no more than 168 hours (7 days)

There are limits to the number of TENs you can apply for in a year and for the same premises.

Warning You could be fined if you make any false statements in your application, or face prosecution if you breach the terms of the notice.
